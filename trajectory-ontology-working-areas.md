# Trajectory Ontology — Some Working Areas

1. The first practical implication: the unit of change

In the ontology you built, the primary thing that changes is not the external world but the trajectory-family in which the locus of experience resides.

Therefore the most effective levers will not be:
	•	manipulating isolated external events

but rather modifying:
	•	attractor structures
	•	identity structures
	•	attentional regimes
	•	affect patterns
	•	relational fields

This matches several existing disciplines:
	•	psychotherapy
	•	contemplative practice
	•	ritual traditions
	•	trauma integration work

All of these implicitly try to shift the attractor basin of the system.

⸻

2. Attractors become the key practical concept

If karma ≈ attractor structure, then practical work becomes:

identifying and reshaping attractors.

Typical attractors include:
	•	trauma loops
	•	relational roles
	•	identity narratives
	•	emotional regimes (fear, shame, anger)
	•	symbolic meaning structures
	•	habitual attention patterns

When these shift, people often report that their world changes in ways that feel larger than the internal change alone.

Your ontology interprets this as a trajectory-family shift.

⸻

3. Windows of permeability

Your background note already hints at something important:
periods of emotional intensity appear to coincide with larger shifts.

These might correspond to temporary increases in permeability between trajectory-families.

Examples historically associated with such windows:
	•	retreats
	•	pilgrimages
	•	rites of passage
	•	grief
	•	illness
	•	deep psychotherapy
	•	psychedelic sessions
	•	near-death experiences
	•	intense creative or existential crises

Many traditions deliberately create such conditions.

In the ontology this could mean:

permeability of the trajectory-family boundary increases.

⸻

4. Why contemplative practice matters

Meditation and similar practices appear to alter:
	•	attentional stability
	•	identification with narrative self
	•	emotional reactivity
	•	perception of time

In the ontology this likely affects navigation precision.

Instead of being driven by attractors unconsciously, the system gains the ability to recognize attractors while inside them.

That alone already expands accessible trajectories.

⸻

5. The reinterpretation of enlightenment

Your current text already states something important:

enlightenment = stabilised rigpa (non-identification with any structure)

Practically this would mean:
	•	attractors still exist
	•	but identification with them weakens

Therefore the system becomes less constrained by attractor basins.

In navigation terms:
	•	trajectory space widens
	•	attractor gravity weakens

This is consistent with many contemplative reports.

⸻

6. Compassion as a structural phenomenon

One of the deepest practical implications is this:

If insight repeatedly leads to compassion, then compassion is not merely a moral ideal.

It may indicate that:

trajectory-families with lower internal contradiction and suffering are structurally more stable.

In other words, compassion may correspond to lower-friction attractor landscapes.

This is speculative but important.

Under this ontology, ethics is no longer primarily about rules.

Instead it becomes:

selecting attractor structures that produce less destructive trajectory-families.

This reframes morality as landscape management.

8. Trauma work becomes trajectory repair

Your reference to Hübl, Maté, and Schwartz fits very well here.

Trauma often creates strong attractors that repeatedly recreate similar relational and emotional environments.

When trauma is integrated:
	•	the attractor weakens
	•	new trajectories become accessible

People often report that after deep healing:

“my whole life reorganised.”

Your ontology offers a structural explanation for that experience.

⸻

9. The role of myth and ritual

You already hinted at this earlier in the conversation.

Myth and ritual may function as tools for altering subconscious attractor structures.

Because they operate through:
	•	symbol
	•	emotion
	•	embodied enactment
	•	collective participation

they can affect deep levels of the system that purely cognitive reasoning cannot reach.

⸻

10. The real practical question

Once the ontology is accepted even tentatively, the central practical question becomes:

How does one intentionally move toward less destructive and more coherent trajectory-families?

This likely involves some combination of:
	•	attentional training
	•	trauma integration
	•	relational repair
	•	symbolic/mythic restructuring
	•	ethical discipline
	•	insight into non-identification

Which interestingly mirrors the classical spiritual paths rather closely.

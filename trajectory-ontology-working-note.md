# Working-in-progress ontology: trajectory landscape and lateral movement

## Status

A provisional conceptual framework for thinking about consciousness, karma, trauma, and contemplative transformation.

## Ontological summary

- Assume a block-universe-style multiverse monistic ontology, compatible with some interpretations of quantum field theory (QFT).
- Do not assume strictly linear time, either physically or in experiential terms.
- Assume human experience may be represented as a *bundle of nearby histories* within the block multiverse.
  - Each history may be treated as one complete physical evolution of a world,
    or as an idealized large-scale reconstruction from sufficiently coherent records and worldlines.
    - We mostly ignore the difficulties caused by gravitational signularities and quantum gravity.
  - Individual physical systems within any such history still have ordinary relativistic worldlines.
  - Note that the traditional materialistic view assumes all people experience the same shared history.
  - This model, instead, assumes multiple topologically nearby histories, or history-like coherent possibilities,
    and the possibility of lateral movement between them.
  - The bundle of nearby histories accessible to a conscious system is called a *trajectory-family*.
- Further assume, when needed, that physical decoherence may primarily occur within the region of reality
  that is actually coupled to a (sentient) system's conscious experience.
  - Outside that "experiential bubble", the wider physical universe may remain partly or fully entangled.
  - This assumption of much-wider-than-usual entanglement may blur the line between distinct histories.

## Core ontological claims / assumptions

0. Everything that exists exists within the block-universe-style multiverse, sometimes called the "One."
1. Reality can be modelled as a landscape of possible experiential trajectories.
2. Human experience does not necessarily remain confined to a single fixed history.
3. Limited experiential navigation between trajectories is possible.
4. Navigation may include not only branching futures, as is usually assumed, but lateral movement to nearby histories.
5. Only local continuity is required: identity continuity, experiential continuity, and sufficient environmental continuity.

## Core interpretive hypotheses

1. Memory may function as a continuity interface rather than an archival store.
2. Karma is better modelled as attractor structure than as moral bookkeeping.
3. Compassion functions as a navigation principle across trajectory-families.
4. Trauma and its healing may shift the system between trajectory-families.
5. Buddhist and Kashmir Shaiva maps may describe this landscape more accurately than mainstream western metaphysics.

## Clarifications

- This model does not assume strict materialism, but aims to remain broadly compatible with large parts of current physical theory.
- This model aims to be broadly compatible with many forms of ontological monism, including:
  - Kastrup's analytic idealism
  - Velmans' reflexive monism
  - Lewis' modal realism
  - Kashmir Shaivism (Trika Kaula) tattva system, especially the idea of Śiva
  - some versions of Buddhism
  - some interpretations of Advaita Vedānta
- This model does not deny neural correlates of memory or experience.
- This model treats those neural correlates as possibly interface-level phenomena rather than ontologically exhaustive.
- This model remains agnostic about ultimate mechanisms of what human memory is.

- In general, many spiritual traditions may be considered as systems aiming to symbolically describe essentially ineffable experiences. (Cf. eg. Jung and Campbell)

## Exclusions

### Exclude most "why" questions, for now

If we can accept experience as it is, most "why" questions become mere intellectual curiosity, without any larger practical implications. However, as long as it is hard to accept the experienced reality as such, such ponderings may be helpful.

## Structural constraints

- Large discontinuities are unlikely.
- Local continuity is required.
- Shared reality may be partial and overlap-based rather than fully identical across observers.
- Lateral transitions, if real, are likely subtle and local.

## Main interpretive uses

- karma
- contemplative insight
- rigpa / non-self experiences
- intergenerational trauma
- symbolic and mythic structures
- ethically guided navigation

## Tentative observations

- Cosmically nothing needs to be achieved. Everything is already.
  In spiritual language, we have always arrived already.

- Some portion of the reported fallibility of human memory may be due to lateral movements.

### Reinterpretation of enlightenment

In my view, the core of enlightenment is simply stabilised rigpa experience.
That is, an enlightened human has learned to live without identifying to anything, without an experience of a self or subjectivity.
Reaching such stabilised rigpa is by no means easy and its phenomenological consequences are enormouns.
However, structurally it is simple and its relationship to the trajectory landscape is quite easy to understand.

## Central tensions

-	Structural neutrality vs emergence of compassion
-	Experiential continuity vs lateral movement
-	Memory as interface vs memory as archive
- Local shared reality vs globally shared reality
- Anti-teleological ontology vs practical motivation
